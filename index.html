Getting Started

Everything you need to know to get started with FlowDeck.

How to invite teammates to a workspace

Go to Settings → Team and click Invite Member. Enter your teammate's email address and select their role — Admin, Editor, or Viewer. They'll receive an invite link by email, valid for 48 hours. Admins can resend invites from the same settings page.

How to archive a completed project board

Open the board, click the ⋯ menu in the top-right corner, and select Archive Board. Archived boards are hidden from your main view but remain fully searchable. You can restore them at any time from Settings → Archived Boards.

Forgot username — how to recover account access

On the FlowDeck login page, click Forgot username? and enter the email address associated with your account. You'll receive an email with your username within a few minutes. If you no longer have access to that email address, contact support@flowdeck.io with proof of account ownership.

How do I set up an automation trigger?

Open your board and click Automations in the top toolbar. Click + Add Automation, then choose a trigger (e.g., "When status changes to Done") and an action (e.g., "Send an email notification"). Review the preview and click Activate. Automations run immediately once active.

Duplicate email notifications after re-enabling automation

This is a known issue: when an automation is re-enabled, any items that already match the trigger condition fire once immediately. To avoid this, temporarily set affected items to a neutral status before re-enabling, then update them after the automation is live.

Slack integration double-posting on status change

Double-posting typically happens when both a Slack integration and an automation are configured for the same trigger. Check Integrations → Slack and Automations to confirm only one is active for that event. Remove or disable the duplicate, then test with a single status change.

CSV import mis-mapping date columns (DD/MM vs MM/DD)

During import, FlowDeck defaults to MM/DD/YYYY. If your CSV uses DD/MM/YYYY, select Custom date format on the column-mapping screen and enter DD/MM/YYYY. You can also pre-convert your dates in Excel or Google Sheets before uploading to avoid the issue.

Trello board import stalling at 80% progress

Large Trello boards (500+ cards) can time out during import. The workaround is to export your Trello board in smaller batches — archive completed cards first, export, then import the active cards. Full large-board import support is on our roadmap for Q3 2025.

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)

Go to Settings → Security → Two-Factor Authentication and click Enable. Scan the QR code with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password), enter the 6-digit code to confirm, and save your backup codes somewhere safe. 2FA applies to your account on all devices.

SSO/SAML setup for Enterprise accounts

Enterprise accounts can configure SSO via Settings → Security → Single Sign-On. You'll need your Identity Provider's metadata URL or XML file. Supported IdPs include Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, and OneLogin. After saving the configuration, test with a non-admin account before enforcing SSO for all users.

Reset a lost 2FA device or backup codes

If you lose access to your authenticator app, use one of your saved backup codes on the login screen. If you no longer have backup codes, an account Admin can reset 2FA from Settings → Team → [your name] → Reset 2FA. For Enterprise accounts with SSO enforced, contact your IdP administrator to restore access.

Enforce 2FA for all workspace members

Admins on Pro and Enterprise plans can require 2FA for every member. Go to Settings → Security → Authentication Policy, enable Require two-factor authentication, and choose a grace period (7, 14, or 30 days). Members who haven't enrolled will see a prompt at next login and cannot access boards until 2FA is enabled.

Password requirements and reset policy

FlowDeck requires passwords of at least 12 characters with a mix of letters and numbers. Passwords are checked against known breach lists at signup and reset. Users can reset a password from the login page via Forgot password? Admins cannot view member passwords but can trigger a reset email from Settings → Team.

Manage active sessions and sign out remotely

View all active sessions under Settings → Security → Active Sessions. Each entry shows device, browser, IP region, and last activity. Click Sign out next to any session to revoke it immediately. Use Sign out everywhere to end all sessions except your current one — useful if you suspect unauthorized access.

Configure session timeout and idle logout

Enterprise admins can set automatic logout after inactivity in Settings → Security → Session Policy. Options range from 15 minutes to 8 hours. When a session expires, unsaved board edits are preserved as drafts and restored on re-login. Mobile sessions follow the same policy unless Remember this device was checked at login.

Create and rotate API keys safely

Generate API keys in Settings → Developers → API Keys. Each key can be scoped to read-only or read-write and limited to specific workspaces. Keys are shown only once at creation — store them in a secrets manager. Rotate keys regularly using Regenerate; the old key stops working immediately after regeneration.

Restrict API access by IP allowlist

Enterprise accounts can limit API calls to approved IP ranges. Go to Settings → Security → IP Allowlist, add CIDR blocks or individual IPs, and enable Enforce for API. Web app access for human users is unaffected unless you also enable Enforce for web login. Changes take effect within 60 seconds.

OAuth app permissions and third-party access

Review connected OAuth apps under Settings → Security → Connected Apps. Each entry shows the app name, granted scopes, and date authorized. Click Revoke to disconnect an app immediately. Admins on Enterprise plans can block new OAuth connections workspace-wide from the same page.

Board-level and item-level permission controls

Control who can view or edit content using board permissions. Open a board, click Share, and assign roles: Owner, Editor, Commenter, or Viewer. For sensitive items, use Lock item to prevent edits without removing view access. Workspace Admins can override board permissions from Settings → Boards.

Private boards and hidden workspaces

Mark a board as Private from the board ⋯ menu → Privacy. Only explicitly invited members can see it in search or sidebar. Private boards still inherit workspace-level Admin access. Enterprise accounts can create Hidden workspaces visible only to assigned members via Settings → Workspaces → Visibility.

Guest access and external collaborator limits

Invite external collaborators as Guests from Settings → Team → Invite Guest. Guests can access only boards they're invited to and cannot create boards or install integrations. Pro plans allow up to 10 guests; Enterprise allows unlimited guests with optional domain restrictions under Settings → Security → Guest Policy.

Data encryption at rest and in transit

All FlowDeck data is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2+ and at rest using AES-256. Database backups are encrypted with separate keys rotated quarterly. Enterprise customers can request a copy of our encryption whitepaper from security@flowdeck.io. We do not offer customer-managed encryption keys (CMEK) at this time.

Export audit logs for compliance reviews

Enterprise admins can export audit logs from Settings → Security → Audit Log. Logs capture login events, permission changes, board exports, API key usage, and admin actions. Filter by date range and event type, then download as CSV or stream to a SIEM via our Splunk and Datadog integrations. Logs are retained for 365 days on Enterprise plans.

Set up SCIM provisioning with your IdP

Enterprise accounts can automate user provisioning via SCIM 2.0. In Settings → Security → SCIM Provisioning, copy the SCIM endpoint URL and bearer token into your IdP (Okta, Azure AD, or Google Workspace). Users are created, updated, and deactivated automatically based on IdP group membership. Test with a small group before syncing your full directory.

Domain verification and email takeover prevention

Verify your company domain in Settings → Security → Domain Verification by adding a DNS TXT record. Once verified, only users with matching email domains can be invited unless an Admin explicitly allows external addresses. This prevents unauthorized signups using your company domain and enables Just-in-Time (JIT) SSO provisioning.

Report a suspected security incident

If you believe your account has been compromised, immediately use Sign out everywhere under Active Sessions, reset your password, and re-enroll 2FA. Report the incident to security@flowdeck.io with your account email, approximate time of the incident, and any suspicious activity observed. Our security team responds within 4 business hours for Enterprise accounts.

GDPR data subject requests (access and deletion)

Users in the EU can request a copy of their personal data or account deletion under GDPR. Submit a request via Settings → Privacy → Data Request or email privacy@flowdeck.io. Access exports are delivered within 30 days as a downloadable archive. Deletion requests permanently remove personal data within 30 days; board content you created may be anonymized rather than deleted if shared with other members.

Disable file downloads on shared boards

Prevent members from downloading attachments on sensitive boards. Open the board, go to ⋯ menu → Board Settings → Security, and enable Disable file downloads. Viewers can still preview files in the browser but cannot save copies. This setting applies to all attachments on the board and cannot be overridden per item.

Watermark exported PDFs and reports

Enterprise admins can add user-identifying watermarks to PDF exports. Enable this in Settings → Security → Export Policy → Watermark PDF exports. Exported files include the downloader's name, email, and timestamp in the footer. This helps trace leaked documents back to the source without blocking exports entirely.

Configure login alerts for new devices

Receive an email when your account is accessed from a new device or location. Enable alerts under Settings → Security → Login Notifications. Each alert includes device type, browser, approximate location, and a This wasn't me link that signs out all sessions and prompts a password reset. Alerts cannot be disabled on Enterprise accounts with enforced security policies.

Block legacy authentication methods

Enterprise admins can disable password-only login when SSO is configured. In Settings → Security → Authentication Policy, enable Require SSO for all members. Members attempting password login will be redirected to your IdP. Keep at least one break-glass Admin account with password + 2FA access in case of IdP outage.

Secure webhook endpoints with signing secrets

When configuring outbound webhooks in Settings → Developers → Webhooks, FlowDeck signs each payload with an HMAC-SHA256 secret. Verify the X-FlowDeck-Signature header on your server before processing events. Rotate the signing secret from the webhook settings page; the previous secret remains valid for 24 hours after rotation.

Penetration test and security questionnaire requests

Enterprise customers can request our latest penetration test summary and completed security questionnaires (SIG Lite, CAIQ). Email security@flowdeck.io from your company domain with your account ID. Standard response time is 5 business days. We do not allow customer-directed penetration testing against production without prior written approval.

Recover a compromised API key

If an API key is exposed, revoke it immediately in Settings → Developers → API Keys → Revoke. Review the audit log for unauthorized API activity in the exposure window. Generate a new key with minimal required scopes and update your integrations. Enable IP allowlisting to reduce future exposure risk. Report confirmed misuse to security@flowdeck.io.

Compliance certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)

FlowDeck maintains SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications, renewed annually. Enterprise customers can request the latest audit reports under NDA via security@flowdeck.io. A public trust center summary is available at flowdeck.io/trust. Certifications cover our production infrastructure, change management, and access control processes.

Request to transfer account ownership to new admin

The current account owner must go to Settings → Account → Ownership Transfer, select the new owner from the member list, and confirm with their password. The new owner will receive a confirmation email. Note: ownership transfer is immediate and cannot be undone without the new owner's cooperation.

Mobile app keeps logging out on Android

This is a known issue affecting Android 13+ devices with aggressive battery optimization enabled. Go to your Android Settings → Apps → FlowDeck → Battery and set it to Unrestricted. If the issue persists, try clearing the app cache. A fix is included in the next mobile release (v4.2.1).

Annual renewal charge — why was I billed now?

FlowDeck subscriptions renew automatically on the same date each year. You can find your renewal date in Settings → Billing → Subscription. A reminder email is sent 14 days before renewal. If you were charged unexpectedly, check that your billing email matches your notification preferences.

Annual vs monthly billing — upgrade charge confusion

When you upgrade from monthly to annual mid-cycle, you're charged the annual rate immediately and your remaining monthly credit is applied as a prorated deduction. The exact amount credited is shown in the upgrade confirmation screen before you confirm.

Refund request within 14-day money-back window

FlowDeck offers a full refund within 14 days of your initial purchase or annual renewal. Email support@flowdeck.io with your account email and the charge date. Refunds are processed within 5–10 business days to your original payment method.
